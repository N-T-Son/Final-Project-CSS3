Fun Facts About Bunnies & Hares

Bunnies and hares belong to the Leporidae family and are known for their strong hind legs and long ears.

Unlike rabbits, hares are born with fur and open eyes. They are also more solitary and live above ground.

Bunnies are very social animals and enjoy the company of other rabbits. They communicate using body language and soft sounds.

Both bunnies and hares have nearly 360-degree vision, helping them detect predators from almost any angle.

Rabbits can turn their ears 180 degrees to pinpoint sounds, which is essential for their survival in the wild.
